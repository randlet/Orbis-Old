Quick Start Guide

Here is a quick walk through of the steps to calculate the Simple Huckel Molecular Orbitals of Pyrrole with Orbis. Pyrrole is a 5 membered ring system with the formula C4H4NH. Since we are only considering the pi-bond system we can ignore the H atoms and just draw the carbons and nitrogens.

Start off by selecting the C atom type from the drop down list:


and then add 4 carbon atoms to the sketch pad by left-clicking in the white sketch pad area.

Next add a nitrogen molecule by selecting the N3 atom type from the drop down list. You will notice that there is both an N2 and and an N3 atom type. The digit following the chemical symbol is the coordination number (the number of atoms that are attached to it). In this case the N is attached to 3 other atoms (2 C's and the H that we are ignoring) so use the N3 atom type.

You can now add bonds to the molecule by left-clicking on an atom and while holding the button down, drag the mouse to another atom and release it. You should see a red line attaching the two atoms now. Join up the 5 atoms so that a ring is formed and then have Orbis automatically "clean up" your sketch a bit by pressing the "Redraw" button on the left:

You should have a molecule that looks something like the following:

Notice that while you were drawing the molecule Orbis was filling in the Huckel determinant with the appropriate values,


creating an energy level diagram and molecular orbital plot,


and calculating the eigenvalues, eigenvectors, pi-bond orders, charge densities as well as atom-atom & atom-bond polarizabilities.


Clicking on one of the other energy level lines in the energy level diagram (or alternatively one of the rows of the eigenvector matrix) will show you what the molecular orbital looks like for that energy level.
