Orbis Help

The Molecule Sketch Pad and Huckel Determinant

Drawing and deleting atoms and bonds

To draw an atom: Use the dropdown atom type list to select the atom type that you wish to add and then click on the Molecule Sketch Pad. Atoms are labled with their atomic symbol and their atom number.

As you add more molecules the Huckel determinant will grow in size and the appropriate value for the hX parameter will be placed along the diagonal. For example if the third atom that you add is of type N3 (nitrogen with a coordination number of 3) than the Huckel determinant at position (3,3) will be given a value of -1.37.[1]

To create a bond: Click on an atom and hold the mouse button down while moving the mouse to another atom. Releasing the mouse on another atom will create a bond between the two. Orbis will automatically determine the type of bond and set the value of the Huckel determinant at (i,j), where i and j are the atom numbers involved in the bond, to the appropriate value of kX-Y.

To delete an atom or bond: Right clicking on an atom or bond will remove it from the molecule. When you delete an atom, any bonds assosciated with that atom will also be deleted.

Changing atom and bond types

You can change the type of any atom or bond (or just the value of hX and kX-Y) by double clicking on an atom or bond. This will bring up a dialog that will allow you to choose the new atom/bond type and its hX/kX-Y value. The figure below shows how you would convert one of the carbon atoms in a benzene ring to N2 to create a pyridine ring. Of course hX and kX-Y values can also be directly edited in the Huckel determinant.

Redrawing the canvas

After drawing a molecule you can use the Redraw button to tidy up the canvas. The figure below shows a molecule before and after a redraw.

Zooming and rotating the canvas

The molecule sketch pad can be zoomed or rotated by using the mouse scroll wheel or holding down Ctrl and using the scroll wheel respectively. The + and - keys can also be used to zoom in or out while holding down Ctrl key in combination with the same keys will rotate the plot.
